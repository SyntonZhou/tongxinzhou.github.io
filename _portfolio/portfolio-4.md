**LAV** is a project on a **LoongArch-based accelerator architecture** for real-time visual inference workloads.

The goal of this work was to explore how a customized hardware architecture can better support vision-oriented inference tasks through closer integration of **instruction-set considerations, accelerator structure, and implementation flow**.

## My involvement

I was involved in multiple parts of the project, including:

- coding and functional implementation,
- circuit design,
- board debugging,
- simulation testing,
- and live system demonstration.

## Why this project is meaningful

This project was especially valuable because it was not limited to theoretical design. It required us to confront the engineering realities of hardware integration, bring-up and debugging, validation under real constraints, and communicating system design clearly in a competitive setting.

For me, this was an important step toward understanding how **computer architecture ideas become implementable systems**.

## Outcome

The project received **Second Prize in the South China Region** at the **8th National College Student IC Innovation and Entrepreneurship Competition**.

More importantly, it strengthened my interest in **accelerator architecture, workload specialization, and engineering-driven system validation**.
