**HW/SW Co-Design (FPGA & ASIC) • Hardware-Friendly Dataflows • Model Compression**

Hi! I am **Tongxin Zhou**, a **first-year M.S. student** at the **School of Integrated Circuits, Nanjing University (NJU)**, advised by **Assoc. Prof. (Research Fellow) Yuxiang Fu**.  
My research aims to make **large models more efficient, deployable, and hardware-friendly** by combining **hardware-aware model compression** (e.g., quantization, structured sparsity, and pruning) with **architecture and dataflow co-design**, and by validating these ideas through **FPGA/RTL engineering prototypes**.

I am particularly interested in bridging the gap between **algorithm design** and **hardware implementation**, with a focus on **efficient inference for large models** under practical deployment constraints.

## Research Interests
I am broadly interested in **AI accelerator architecture** and **hardware/software co-design** for:
- **Efficient LLM inference**, especially under tight memory and bandwidth constraints
- **Edge AI and embodied intelligence**, where latency, energy efficiency, and robustness are critical
- **Hardware-friendly algorithms and kernels**, including dataflow mapping, tiling/scheduling, and memory hierarchy optimization
- **FPGA prototyping and RTL implementation**, with an engineering-first mindset toward reproducibility and measurable system-level gains

## Selected Projects (Engineering & Research)
- **FPGA Hardware Processor for Monocular Depth Estimation (Robotic Vision)**  
  Explored **quantization strategies** and designed an **FPGA acceleration pipeline** for edge deployment; current work focuses on **real-time streaming** and reducing end-to-end latency.

- **LAV: LoongArch-Based Real-Time Image Task Processing Network Accelerator** *(IC Innovation & Entrepreneurship Competition)*  
  Proposed and implemented a customized accelerator architecture for real-time vision workloads; contributed to **coding, circuit design, board bring-up, simulation, and live demonstrations**.

- **Low-Power, High-Resolution ΔΣ Modulator for MEMS Capacitive Accelerometers** *(Team Lead)*  
  Designed and validated a delta-sigma modulator achieving **16-bit effective resolution**, **1.67 μW** power consumption, and **101 dB** quantization SNR.

- **Chinese LLM Construction via Parameter Fusion** *(Undergraduate Research Program)*  
  Conducted iterative experiments on **model parameter fusion/merging** for Chinese large language models and evaluated performance trends under advisor guidance.
