Zhou Tongxin, a graduate representative and a master's student of the 2025 cohort, starting from the perspective of scientific research mission and era responsibility, emphasized that research should be rooted in real needs, inherit the discipline's history, and respond to national strategies. Combining his own experience in participating in the research and development of intangible cultural heritage protection chips, he proposed that scientific research needs to connect technology with humanities, focus on breaking through full-stack capabilities, and serve national industrial needs. He called on all students to cherish the platform resources of Suzhou Campus of Nanjing University, contribute their efforts in basic research, technology implementation or ecological construction, and truly write a generation's answer to solving the "bottleneck" problems.
