This project investigated the design of a **low-power, high-resolution sigma-delta modulator** for **MEMS capacitive accelerometers** under microwatt-level power constraints.

Unlike my FPGA and AI-accelerator projects, this work trained me at the **circuit and mixed-signal design level**, where accuracy, power, and noise performance must be balanced carefully through detailed modeling and design choices.

## My role

I served as the **team leader** and was responsible for:

- parameter modeling,
- circuit design,
- report writing,
- and the final project defense.

## Outcome

The project achieved:

- **16-bit effective resolution**,
- **1.67 μW power consumption**,
- **101 dB quantization SNR**.

It was also recognized as an **excellent undergraduate innovation project**.

## Why this project matters

I value this work because it strengthened my understanding of how rigorous hardware design is built from first principles. It also gave me a deeper appreciation of the trade-offs among **precision, power, and implementation complexity**, which continue to shape how I think about efficient computing systems more broadly.

Although my current research is more focused on AI systems and accelerators, this project remains important in my development because it reflects a strong foundation in **device-aware and circuit-level hardware thinking**.
