This project focuses on **hardware acceleration for monocular depth estimation** in robotic vision systems, with the goal of enabling efficient inference on edge platforms.

Monocular depth estimation is attractive for embodied and robotic applications because it can provide spatial understanding with relatively simple sensing hardware. However, deploying such models in real time on resource-limited devices requires careful co-design across **model representation, quantization strategy, and hardware architecture**.

## What I did

In this project, I:

- studied and evaluated **quantization strategies** for the target model,
- developed corresponding **hardware acceleration solutions** on FPGA,
- participated in the construction of a **multi-layer depth-estimation hardware prototype**,
- worked toward more practical **real-time edge deployment**.

## Technical significance

What makes this project important to me is that it sits at the interface I care most about: **machine learning models meeting real hardware constraints**.

Compared with pure software benchmarking, FPGA prototyping exposed issues such as operator mapping overhead, dataflow efficiency, resource bottlenecks, and deployment-oriented trade-offs between precision and throughput.

## Broader relevance

This work connects naturally to my broader research interests in **edge AI, embodied intelligence, hardware/software co-design, and FPGA/RTL-based validation of efficient inference systems**.
